HabitCore Privacy Policy

Effective and last updated: July 15, 2026
HabitCore is a habit, task, routine, and consistency tracking app. This policy explains which information remains on your device, which technical information may be processed by advertising services, and the privacy choices available to you.

1. Scope and data controller

This policy applies to the HabitCore mobile application. The HabitCore developer is responsible for the app's data practices. Contact details are provided in the "Contact us" section below and in HabitCore's Google Play listing.

2. Information stored locally

HabitCore stores the information you create locally on your device. This can include plans, goals, tasks, schedules, reminders, categories, notes, links, completion history, streaks, focus sessions, skip and delay history, achievements, preferences, and generated insights.

HabitCore does not upload this user-entered tracking content to a HabitCore account or HabitCore-operated server. HabitCore does not attach this content to advertising requests.

3. Advertising and Google AdMob

HabitCore uses the Google Mobile Ads SDK and Google AdMob to display advertisements. Depending on your region, settings, and consent choices, ads may be personalized, non-personalized, or subject to limited or restricted data processing.

The Google Mobile Ads SDK may automatically collect and share:

  • IP address, which may be used to estimate a device's general location.
  • App and advertising interactions, such as app launches, ad views, taps, and video views.
  • Diagnostic and performance information, such as SDK performance, launch time, hangs, crashes, and energy usage.
  • Device or account-related identifiers, including the Android Advertising ID, App Set ID, and other identifiers where applicable.

Google and participating advertising partners may use this information for ad delivery, personalization where permitted, measurement, analytics, security, and fraud prevention. Data transmitted by the Google Mobile Ads SDK is encrypted in transit.

HabitCore does not sell your plans, goals, tasks, notes, completion history, or backup content to advertisers.

4. Consent and privacy messages

HabitCore uses Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) to request and store advertising privacy choices where required. HabitCore checks whether ads may be requested before loading an ad.

EEA, United Kingdom, and Switzerland

Users in these regions may receive a European regulations message that explains advertising purposes and vendors and asks for consent where required. You may accept, reject, or manage the available purposes and vendors. Withdrawing consent does not affect processing that occurred before withdrawal.

Applicable United States jurisdictions

Users covered by applicable U.S. state privacy laws may use the "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" and targeted advertising controls made available through the U.S. state regulations message. Choices may be communicated using the IAB Global Privacy Platform (GPP) signal and may cause restricted data processing.

Changing your choice

When required for your region, open HabitCore and go to Settings > Privacy > Ad privacy choices. You can also manage or delete the Android Advertising ID through your device's privacy and ads settings.

5. Device storage and advertising technologies

The app and its advertising SDK may use local device storage to retain consent signals, privacy preferences, fraud-prevention information, frequency controls, and advertising identifiers. Mobile apps do not use browser cookies in exactly the same way as websites, but these technologies can serve similar storage and recognition purposes.

This privacy-policy page does not contain AdMob ad tags or consent scripts.

6. Export, import, and sharing

HabitCore allows you to export, import, copy, or share plans and full backups. HabitCore performs these actions only when you request them. Files sent through email, messaging, cloud storage, or another app are then processed under that third party's privacy policy. You are responsible for choosing recipients and protecting exported files.

7. Ask AI and external services

HabitCore can prepare prompts or structured plan content for external AI services. HabitCore does not automatically send this content. When you choose an AI provider, copy a prompt, or share content to another app, the selected content is processed according to that provider's terms and privacy policy. Review prompts before sharing if they contain information you consider private.

8. Data retention and deletion

Locally stored HabitCore data remains on your device until you delete it, clear the app's storage, restore a different backup, or uninstall the app. Exported copies remain wherever you placed or shared them.

Advertising information is retained by Google and advertising partners according to their own policies and legal obligations. HabitCore does not control their independent retention periods.

9. Your privacy rights and controls

Depending on your location, your rights may include:

  • Accessing, correcting, exporting, or deleting local app content.
  • Withdrawing advertising consent where consent applies.
  • Opting out of sale, sharing, or targeted advertising where applicable.
  • Resetting or deleting your Android Advertising ID in device settings.
  • Contacting the developer about a privacy request or concern.

Because HabitCore's tracking content is stored locally and is not held in a HabitCore account, the developer generally cannot retrieve or delete that content remotely. Use the controls in the app or on your device.

10. Children's privacy

HabitCore is not designed to knowingly collect children's personal information on HabitCore-operated servers. Parents or guardians who believe a child's information has been processed improperly should use the contact method below. Advertising configuration and age treatment must follow the app's published target-audience settings and applicable law.

11. Security and international processing

Local data is protected by the security controls of your device. No storage or transmission method is completely secure. Google and its advertising partners may process advertising information in countries other than your own, subject to their stated safeguards and applicable law.

12. Third-party privacy information

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • How Google uses information from apps that use its services
  • Google My Ad Center

13. Policy changes

This policy may be updated when HabitCore's features, advertising providers, legal requirements, or data practices change. The updated version will show a revised effective date. Material changes may also be communicated in the app or store listing where appropriate.

14. Contact us

For privacy questions or requests, contact the HabitCore developer using the developer contact email displayed on HabitCore's Google Play listing.
